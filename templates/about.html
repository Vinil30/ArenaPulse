Mission
News should inform, not overwhelm.

Chronicle was built on a single conviction: the world produces far more signal than any human can process. We exist to close that gap — not by simplifying, but by intelligently curating.

Every hour, our agent scans thousands of sources across politics, technology, science, culture, and finance. It reads, evaluates, and composes — surfacing the stories that shape the moment and presenting them without noise or agenda.

How It Works
01
Scan
The agent continuously monitors hundreds of trusted news sources, RSS feeds, and wire services across every major beat.
02
Evaluate
Stories are scored by relevance, recency, and significance. Duplicates are merged. Noise is filtered. Only the essential remains.
03
Compose
Each article is rewritten for clarity and concision — preserving the facts, stripping the filler, and sourcing back to the original.
04
Deliver
Stories appear in the Journal within minutes of breaking. Ask follow-up questions via the Chat assistant at any time.
Principles
◈
Transparency
Every article links directly to its original source. We don't obscure provenance — we celebrate it. You can always verify what we report.
◇
Neutrality
Chronicle draws from sources across the political and ideological spectrum. The agent is instructed to report, not editorialize — to present facts, not conclusions.
○
Clarity
Complex stories deserve simple language. We write for comprehension first. No jargon. No padding. No word wasted.
△
Currency
The world doesn't wait. Neither do we. The agent runs continuously so that when news breaks, Chronicle is ready within the hour.
